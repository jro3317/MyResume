(2015)

Nomination and Finalist for the International Dyslexia Association (IDA) Remy Johnston Award

(2015)

The IDA Remy Johnston Award is presented each year to an individual student (high school or college age) with dyslexia who "refuses to be limited by the challenges of learning differences, strives for excellence, chooses to live life as an achiever and role model for others, and continues to enrich the lives of families, friends, employers and the community in which s/he lives through service." This year, I was nominated by 11 people in our community, which included the the President of the District of Columbia's IDA branch, our Arlington Public School's Director of Special Education, my Special Education teacher and many other parents that have gotten to know me from various dyslexia awareness and training sessions during which I have presented information about dyslexia and Assistive Technology. Decoding Dyslexia Capital Hill Days

(July 2015)

Decoding Dyslexia is a network of parent-led grassroots movements across the country concerned with limited access to educational instruction for dyslexic students in public education. Since my sister and I founded More Than Dyslexics, I've had the opportunity to network with our Decoding Dyslexia's Virginia Chapter over the past two years. In addition to a few local awareness and support group meetings, I have attended three formal Congressional Hearings to support new legislation. In July 2015, Decoding Dyslexia arranged their second annual Capital Hill Days event. Representatives from each of the 50 Decoding Dyslexia states gathered on Capital Hill to advocate for legislative changes to help support students in public schools with dyslexia. I had the opportunity to meet with Congressman Dave Brat (VA), the legislative assistant from Congressman Don Beyer's office (VA) and the legislative assistant from Congressman Scott Perry's office (PA).

Multi Modal Transportations and Student Safety Special Committee

(January 2013 - June 2014)

In September 2012, Arlington Public Schools reconfigured our school bus routes and service, and as a result, hundreds of students were no longer eligible for bus transportation. Due to the community concern for safety and transportation options for students that erupted, the Arlington School Board established the Multimodal Transportation and Student Safety Special Committee (MMTSSSC) to report to the School Board to provide recommendations regarding the safe transportation of students to and from school by a variety of modes of transportation.

The overarching goal of the MTSSSC is to ensure student safety for all forms of transportation, including but not limited to walking, bus transportation, bicycling, and driving. Additional goals are to provide on-time bus service; to maximize efficient use of APS transportation resources; to ensure a reasonable walk distance to each school; to support effective traffic management at school sites; to make informed decisions that reflect the APS dedication to environmental stewardship; and to consider and report budget implications for any Committee recommendations.

The MMTSSC met approximately every two weeks for 18 months, and presented a Recommending Report to our School Board in June 2014 (linked here)

There were 13 Voting Committee Members from the Community appointed to the MMTSSSC, which included two students (click here to see my letters of recomendation to be appointed to the committee), and eight additional APS and Arlington County Staff who supported the Committee's work, which included the Arlington County Director of Transportation and the APS Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Operations.
